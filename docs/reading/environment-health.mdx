## Passage 1: Plastic Pollution and Its Solutions

Plastic pollution has become one of the most pressing **environmental** issues of our time. Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste end up in oceans, harming marine life and **contaminating** the food chain.

Single-use plastics — such as bags, straws, and bottles — are the biggest **contributors** to this problem. These items are used for just a few minutes but can take hundreds of years to **decompose** in nature. As a result, many countries have **banned** or restricted the use of single-use plastics and **encouraged** consumers to switch to **reusable** alternatives.

On an individual level, simple actions can make a big difference. Bringing your own shopping bag, using a reusable water bottle, and properly **sorting** your trash for recycling are all effective ways to reduce plastic waste. While the problem is **enormous**, collective action can lead to **meaningful** change.

## Passage 2: The Benefits of Regular Exercise

Regular physical exercise is one of the most effective ways to maintain good health. Research has **consistently** shown that exercise **reduces** the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, **diabetes**, and certain types of cancer.

Beyond physical health, exercise also has significant benefits for **mental** well-being. Physical activity **stimulates** the release of **endorphins** — chemicals in the brain that improve mood and reduce feelings of stress and anxiety. Even a 20-minute walk can make a noticeable difference in how you feel.

Despite these well-known benefits, many people **struggle** to exercise regularly. Common **barriers** include lack of time, motivation, and access to **facilities**. Health experts recommend finding activities that you genuinely enjoy, setting **realistic** goals, and **incorporating** exercise into your daily routine — such as walking to work or taking the stairs instead of the elevator.

## Passage 3: Food Safety and Healthy Eating

Food safety is a growing concern in many countries. Issues such as **pesticide** **residues**, food **additives**, and **contamination** during processing can pose serious health risks to consumers.

To protect themselves, consumers should pay attention to food labels and choose products from **reputable** sources. Washing fruits and vegetables **thoroughly** before eating, cooking meat to the proper **temperature**, and storing food correctly can also reduce the risk of foodborne illness.

In addition to safety, the **quality** of our diet plays a crucial role in long-term health. Nutritionists recommend a **balanced** diet rich in whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins. Reducing the **intake** of processed foods, sugar, and **saturated** fat can help prevent obesity and related diseases. Small changes in eating habits can lead to **substantial** improvements in overall health.

## Passage 4: Air Quality and Public Health

Air pollution is a major public health **threat** that affects billions of people worldwide. According to the World Health Organization, outdoor air pollution causes an **estimated** 4.2 million **premature** deaths each year.

The main sources of air pollution include vehicle **emissions**, industrial activity, and the burning of fossil fuels for energy. In many cities, **particulate** matter and harmful gases such as nitrogen **dioxide** reach levels that far **exceed** safe **limits**, especially during winter months.

Individuals can take steps to **minimize** their **exposure** to air pollution. Checking daily air quality reports, **avoiding** outdoor exercise during high-pollution periods, and using air **purifiers** at home can all help. At a **broader** level, transitioning to clean energy sources, improving public **transportation**, and enforcing **stricter** emission **standards** are essential for **tackling** this global health crisis.

## Passage 5: Mental Health Awareness

Mental health has gained **increasing** attention in recent years, as people begin to recognize that **psychological** well-being is just as important as physical health. Conditions such as depression, anxiety, and stress affect millions of people across all age groups and **backgrounds**.

One of the biggest **obstacles** to addressing mental health issues is **stigma**. Many people are **reluctant** to seek help because they fear being judged or **misunderstood**. This is particularly true in cultures where mental health problems are seen as a sign of weakness rather than a medical condition.

Increasing awareness and **reducing** stigma requires effort at every level of society. Schools can teach students about emotional health from a young age. Workplaces can provide mental health resources and create supportive **environments**. And individuals can help by being **compassionate** listeners and encouraging those who are struggling to seek **professional** help. By **normalizing** conversations about mental health, we can create a society where everyone feels safe to ask for support.
