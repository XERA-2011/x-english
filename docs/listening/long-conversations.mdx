## 1. 学术讨论 Academic Discussion

<AudioPlayer 
  src="/audio/listening/long-conv-01.mp3"
  transcript={`
**Student:** Professor Johnson, I'm having trouble with my research paper on climate change.
**Professor:** What seems to be the problem?
**Student:** Well, I've collected a lot of data, but I'm not sure how to organize it effectively.
**Professor:** I see. Have you tried creating an outline first? That usually helps structure your arguments.
**Student:** I did make an outline, but it feels too scattered. There are so many aspects to cover.
**Professor:** Climate change is indeed a broad topic. Perhaps you should narrow your focus. What aspect interests you most?
**Student:** I'm particularly interested in the impact on coastal cities.
**Professor:** Excellent! That's much more manageable. Focus on that, and your paper will be more coherent. Also, make sure to include recent case studies.
**Student:** That's really helpful. Should I schedule another meeting after I revise my outline?
**Professor:** Yes, let's meet next Wednesday. Bring your revised outline and we'll go through it together.
  `}

## 3. 旅行计划 Travel Planning

<AudioPlayer 
  src="/audio/listening/long-conv-03.mp3"
  transcript={`
**Woman:** So, have you thought about where we should go for our weekend trip?
**Man:** I've been looking at a few options. How about the mountains? There's great hiking this time of year.
**Woman:** Hmm, I was actually thinking about the beach. We could relax and enjoy the sun.
**Man:** The beach sounds nice, but the weather forecast says it might rain this weekend.
**Woman:** Oh, that's disappointing. What about the mountains then? Is there good accommodation?
**Man:** Yes, there's a cozy lodge that got excellent reviews. It's about a three-hour drive from here.
**Woman:** Three hours? That's quite far. Do we have to drive, or is there another option?
**Man:** We could take a bus, but it takes four hours and only runs twice a day. Driving gives us more flexibility.
**Woman:** You're right. And we can split the driving. What about the cost?
**Man:** The lodge is $120 per night, and we'd need to stay two nights. Gas would be about $50 round trip.
**Woman:** So roughly $290 total. That's reasonable. Let's book it!
**Man:** Great! I'll make the reservation today.
  `}
/>

## 4. 新闻采访 News Interview

<AudioPlayer 
  src="/audio/listening/long-conv-04.mp3"
  transcript={`
**Journalist:** Today we have Dr. Miller, an expert on urban development. Dr. Miller, many people are concerned about the increasing traffic in our city. What's the main cause?
**Dr. Miller:** Well, the rapid population growth is certainly a factor, but the core issue is our outdated public transportation system. It simply hasn't kept up with the city's expansion.
**Journalist:** Are there any immediate plans to solve this?
**Dr. Miller:** The city council has just approved a new budget to expand the subway lines. This project will start next spring and is expected to take three years to complete.
**Journalist:** Three years is a long time. What can commuters do in the meantime?
**Dr. Miller:** We are encouraging companies to adopt flexible working hours. If people don't all travel during the peak hours of 8 AM and 5 PM, it will significantly reduce congestion. Also, carpooling programs are being subsidized.
**Journalist:** Do you think these short-term measures will be effective?
**Dr. Miller:** They won't solve the problem entirely, but our studies show they could reduce traffic by up to 20% if widely adopted. 
**Journalist:** Thank you, Dr. Miller, for your insights.
  `}
